The Witches Guide and Journey

Witchcraft is the practice of magical skills, spells, and abilities. The term is commonly associated with those who use metaphysical means to communicate with spirits, deities or ancestors. The most widely known characteristic of a witch is the ability to cast a spell, which could consisit of a set of words, a formula or verse, a ritual action, or any combination of these. Spells traditionally were cast by many methods, such as the inscription of runes or sigils on an object to give that object magical powers. Wicca, is a modern pagan religion.They share a general set of beliefs and practices. They believe in Goddess, respect nature, and hold both polytheistic and pantheistic views.

Real Origins, Hunts, and Trials

Most early witches were thought to be pagans doing the Devil's work. Many, however, were simply natural healers or so-called "wise women" whose choice of profession was misunderstood. In the west, witchcraft has been more commonly believed to be an ordinary person's free choice to learn and practice magic with the help of the supernatural. In the earliest centuries of human civilization, witches were women who served goddesses and therefore were revered throughout their communities. Trained in the sacred arts and the holiest of rituals, they became known as wise women and may have some of the earliest manifestations of what we now recognize as the witch.

The fear of witches spread to Europe. In the 1300s, when the plague decimated Europe and killed one in three people, it also brought with it hysteria. Many attributed their misfortune to the Devil himself- and his supposed worshipers. The Catholic Church's inquisition, expanded its efforts to seek out and punish the non-catholic causes of the mass deaths, including the Devil-doting witches. In order to save the church and its followers from the Devil, these women had to be 'tamed'. It is with that in mind the Catholic Church inquisitors Jacob Springer and Henrik Kramer wrote the Malleus Maleficarum , a book which assisted witch hunters in the gruesome task of diagnosing and punishing so-called witches.

The Malleus recommended not only torture but also deception in order to obtain confessions. All confessions acquired with the use of torture had to be confirmed, they'd be taken to another place and had to confirm to certify it was not due alone to force of the torture. The Malleus Maleficarum has been blamed for the death of tens of thousands of women and men, and its instructions on the identification, prosecution, and punishment of witches arguably did much to pave the way for the great witch hunts of the 16th and 17th centuries in Europe. The guide is divided into 3 parts, part 1 discusses beliefs about witches and their practices witches were thought to. It also discusses the Biblical and theological basis of witchcraft. Part 2 discusses in detail how witches cause harm and how that harm can be prevented. Part 3 is chiefly concerned with the legal aspects of trying and convicting witches. The authors justified their harsh tactics because it was believed necessary to vigorously persecute witches in order to protect the innocent from the Devil.

Altar Guide and Essentials

An altar is a "raised structure or place used for worship or prayer", upon which a wiccan practioner places several symbolic and functional items for the purpose of worshiping the God and Goddess, casting spells, and/or saying chants and prayers. There are many types of altars Wiccans may choose to use during ritual. Some say wood from an oak tree is best while others argue maple or teak are the only ones allowed. This is because in many circles, different types of wood are believed to carry certain magical qualities. For example, in one wiccan tradition, oak symbolizes great strength and may be used to strengthen the rite they are performing. In another tradition, maple may be seen as the strongest. Whether that be a coffee table or a tree stump, it is up to the Wiccan.

Practitioners may keep various religious items upon the altar, or they may use the altar and the items during their religious workings. According to Scott Cunningham, a popular Wiccan author, the left side of the altar should be considered the Goddess area; feminine or 'yonic' (reproductive/feminine power)symbols such as bowls and chalices. The right side is designated for the God; 'phallic' symbols such as the athame and the wand are placed to the right side. The left and the right associateions vary according to personal preference, but the center area is almost always considered the "both" area, or the working area. In the center of the altar are kept the main symbols of the Wiccan faith, such as the pentacle.

Some Wiccans arrange their altars to represent all four elements and directions. In the North the earth element is represented; in the east is air, in the south is fire, and in the west is water. These elements can be represented in various ways, but generally do not vary in elemental and directional correspondences.

Common items on a wiccan altar include:

Candles
Incense
Wand
Pentacle
Offering bowls
Crystals
Chalice
Cauldron
Athame (Ritual Knife)
Salt/Salt Water

There are eight Wiccan holidays, known as sabbats, that celebrate the cycles and seasons of nature. These include the four seasons (Winter, Spring, Autumn and Summer), the mating habits animals and the reaping and sowing of crops. Based on the Sabbat, the altar is decorated accordingly. For example, the Summer Solstice altar cloth should be white and the altar decorated with summer flowers, fruits and anything else that symbolizes Summer. This goes for each Sabbat. Certain Wiccan traditions may have different colors but universally, the altar is usually decorated to represent the time of year

Pentacle

A pentacle is a talisman that is used in magical evocation, and it usually made of parchment, paper, cloth, or metal (although it can be of other materials), upon which a magical design is drawn. Pentacles are almost always shaped as disks or flat circles.
Gerald Gardner, known by some as the 'Father of Wicca', got his concept of pentacles in large part from the 1909 Rider-Waite tarot deck, in which the pentacles are disks that are covered with a pentagram. Gardner defined a pentacle as a synonym of pentagram.

The pentagram is used as a symbol of faith, a symbol of the 5 elements; Spirit, Air, Earth, Water, and fire (one for each point), and the circle (the universe),contains and connects them all. The top point represents the element of Spirit, the other four points represent the four spirtual elements. The pentacle is seen by some to be a manifestation tool allowing the star to act as a portal for spirits while the circle contains the spirit protecting the summoner and allowing the spirit to remain within our plan longer. On a smaller scale, a pentacle can be used to manifest things you want in your life and charge crystals or other ritual tools with your intent.

When a circle is cast for Wicca or witchcraft rituals, pentacles are often drawn in the air at the cardinal directions to invoke the assistance of the elemental powers:North for Earth, East for Air, West for Water, and South for Fire. Pentacles can also be drawn on windows, door, and magical tools, often with oils or blessed water, to invoke the power and protection of the elemental forces. It is a common practice during home cleansing and blessings to trace a pentacle onto each door or window using salted water for instace. Pentacles may also be combined with other symbols such as runes or stones to focus its power for a specific purpose.

If you choose to wear or use a pentcale, you should decide what it represents to you. Since Wiccans are not held to any formal belief system, it can represent whatever you, your coven, or a specific path you follow. Like all things in Wicca, it should be used with intent and respect, so take some time to decide what the pentacle means to you.

Grimoire and Book of Shadows

A grimoire is a textbook of magic, typically including instructions on how to create magical objects like talismans and amulets, how to perform magical spells, charms and divination, and how to summon or invoke supernatural entities such as angels, spirits, deities and demons. In many cases, the books themselves are believed to be imbued with magical powers.

Most commonly mistaken as a Book of Shadows, a grimoire is not as personal as its counterpart and in fact, a grimoire doesnt contain any personal writings inside. The only contents found in a grimoire would be information on spells, rituals, and the preparation of magical tooks, and lists of ingredients and their magical correspondence.

Types of Witches/Craft

A Wiccan witch is someone who practices witchcraft within the confines of Wicca. They follow the Wiccan Rede and call upon the Lord and the Lady when doing spellwork and ritual.

A Traditional Witch is someone who practices witchcraft within their folklore or "old tradition". These traditions and folklore practices generally pre-date Wicca and vary depending on the area the particular witch lives in and their ancestry.

A Green Witch is someone who uses plants in their witchcraft. They may use herbs and spices in their spellwork, or they may grow a garden. Their witchcraft is predominatly focused around nature and the plants it offers. They feel closest to their witchcraft and practice when they are close to nature.

A Hedge Witch is someone who walks the hedges of reality. Their witchcraft involves travel between worlds, and they practice communication between this world and others regularly. Hedge Witches may practice astral projection and be adept at divination and spirit communication.

A Kitchen Witch is someone whos witchcraft is mainly focused in the kitchen. Kitchen Witches cook their magick, imbuing magical spells, herbs, and potions with the culinary arts.

A Secuar Witch is one who practices any form of witchcraft outside the confines of a religion or spirtual practice.

An Eclectic Witch is one who draws on the many different traditions, practices, and spiritualities available to them to craft an individualized belief system. They do not follow any particular tradition and they tend to make their own rules.
